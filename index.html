Publications

Low-Voltage 9T SRAM in 22 nm CMOS with Optimized Read Path for Reduced Access Time and Enhanced Stability

A novel 9T differential SRAM cell is proposed to address stability and reliability issues of conventional 6T designs at 22 nm and low supply voltages. The architecture introduces an isolated read path, eliminating read disturbance and significantly improving Read Static Noise Margin (RSNM) while maintaining balanced write performance.

The design achieves ~3× faster read access compared to LP8T and UV9T cells at 0.8 V, without compromising write delay or energy efficiency. Robustness is validated through 5000-iteration Monte Carlo simulations accounting for process variations (Vth, channel length, oxide thickness).

Implemented using Synopsys HSPICE (22 nm PTM) and laid out in Microwind, the cell operates efficiently in the 0.72–0.88 V range. At 0.8 V, it delivers ~0.347 ns read delay and ~0.688 ns write delay with improved RSNM.

This design is well-suited for low-power SRAM arrays in AI, IoT, and energy-constrained VLSI applications. DOI: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11396158

High-Speed Data Transfer Using Li-Fi Technology: a Novel Approach for Hard Disk to PC Communication

This paper presents a Li-Fi (Visible Light Communication) based system for data transfer between a hard disk and a personal computer.

The proposed system uses LEDs for optical transmission and photodiodes for reception. Data is converted into binary format, modulated using PWM/OFDM techniques, and transmitted through visible light. On the receiver side, signal conditioning (amplification and filtering), threshold detection, Manchester decoding, and CRC-based error detection are implemented to reconstruct the original data.

A prototype was developed and tested for multimedia data transfer, achieving up to 100 Mbps for text/audio and 500 Mbps for video under controlled laboratory conditions. DOI: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11307853
